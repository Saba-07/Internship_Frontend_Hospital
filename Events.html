BLOOD DONATION CAMP

Nalgonda: Healthcare Hospital Nalgonda Branch will conduct series of blood donation camps starting from Doctors’ Day on July 1. Healthcare Hospital Nalgonda Branch president Dr Vasantha Kumari reminded that lives are lost due to non-availability of blood in the emergency conditions. With a target to make India as blood unit surplus country, the Healthcare Hospital has decided to conduct blood donation camps on the first day of every month throughout the year.
“Every month, a private hospital or nursing home would take responsibility of conducting health camp. On July 1, blood donation camp will be conducted by Keerthi Hospital in Healthcare Hospital hall in Nalgonda town from 9 am to 11 am. Doctors, medical representatives and staff of hospitals would donate blood at the blood donation camp. The general public can also participate in blood donation camp”, she says. In the evening on the Doctors’ Day, national Healthcare Hospital flag would be installed at Healthcare Hospital Hall and doctors will take Healthcare Hospital pledge. Apart from that senior doctors would also be felicitated by Healthcare Hospital on the occasion of Doctor’s Day. Reminding that Healthcare Hospital Nalgonda branch was taking up awareness programmes on health issues and conducting health camps as a social responsibility, she pointed out that the branch has conducted special health camp for HIV positive children, cancer screening camp, Thiro-Health Camp, congenital health defects health camp and health camp for police families so far.

FREE HEALTH CHECK-UP

Healthcare Hospitala Health launched a health campaign across the city, in line with Mother’s Day and World Hypertension Day celebrations. The initiative is marked by Healthcare Hospitala Medical Centre, Langford hosting free health check-up camp for mothers at the hospital and Healthcare Hospitala Health City, Bommasandra conducting free blood pressure check-up camps across the city. The Mother’s Day Celebrations at Healthcare Hospitala Medical Centre, Langford and Healthcare Hospitala Health City, Bommasandra is offering mothers an opportunity to avail free customized health checkup that will include Blood Pressure check, Random Blood Sugar, ECG, Ultrasound Scan, ECHO as well as Doctor Consultation. The camp will be held till 15th June 2018. Mother’s Day free health check-up camp at Healthcare Hospitala Medical Centre Langford Date:-Till 15th June 2018 Tests included in the free health check:-Blood Pressure, Random Blood Sugar, ECG, Ultrasound Scan, ECHO and Doctor Consultation Venue:-Healthcare Hospitala Health City, Bommasandra Healthcare Hospitala Medical Centre, Langford Town Time:-9AM – 5PM Contact details:-186-0208-0208 As part of the World Hypertension Day campaign, Healthcare Hospital Health is conducting a free blood pressure check-up camp across the city. The check-up will be held at Tech Parks, Corporate Offices, Public Parks, Community Halls, Apartments/Housing Societies and Supermarkets in the city. Apart from helping people understand their blood pressure quotient, the campaign is also aimed at creating awareness about the cause and effects of the condition and educating them on how to keep blood pressure under control. The initiative is expected to reach out to more than 3000 people. HEALTH AWARENESS PROGRAMME CONDUCTED FOR SCHOOL CHILDERN

A health awareness programme was conducted for students from a school in Chennai. The children were sensitized about the importance of washing their hands before and after eating and exercising daily and the importance of playing sports and inculcating other healthy habits to avoid falling sick. The children were asked to share their daily routines and the steps they took every day to maintain hygiene. The children learnt a lot from the session and promised to follow all the good habits. Smile on Wheels programme at present has 34 operational projects in 265 remote villages and urban slums across India, directly benefitting over 400,000 lives in a year. Smile Foundation is a national level development organisation directly benefitting over 400,000 children and their families every year, through more than 200 live welfare projects on education, healthcare, livelihood and women empowerment, in over 950 remote villages and slums across 25 states of India.
